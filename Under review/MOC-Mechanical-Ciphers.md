# Mechanical Ciphers — Index

> **Definition:** Mechanical ciphers are implemented using physical devices — disks, rotors, wheels, or other mechanisms — rather than pure pencil-and-paper operations.
> ← Back to [[MOC-Classical-Ciphers]]

---

## Ciphers in This Family

### Disk & Wheel Devices
- [[Alberti Cipher]] — First mechanical cipher; two concentric bronze disks; Leon Battista Alberti, 1467 *(also [[MOC-Polyalphabetic]])*
- [[Cipher Disk]] — Generic cipher wheel; outer ring = plaintext, inner ring = ciphertext; rotate to set key
- [[Jefferson Wheel Cipher]] — 36 wooden wheels on a spindle; Thomas Jefferson, 1795; rediscovered as M-94
- [[Mexican Army Cipher Wheel]] — Military cipher disk; homophonic variant

### Rotor Machines
- [[Enigma Machine]] — German electromechanical rotor cipher; WWII; 3–4 rotors + reflector + plugboard
- [[Lorenz Cipher]] — German teleprinter cipher; more complex than Enigma; broken by Colossus

### Self-Modifying Disk Ciphers
- [[Chaocipher]] — John Byrne's self-modifying cipher disk; invented 1918; never patented; algorithm revealed 2010

### Card-Based
- [[Solitaire Cipher]] — Bruce Schneier's cipher using a standard deck of cards; designed to resist NSA
